About

My interest in software development began during my undergraduate years at the University of Toronto. However, majoring in Electrical Engineering I did not actively pursue it. What I loved about the Electrical and Computer Engineering was the breadth of topics that it covered ranging from electromagnetism and power systems to analog and digital electronics to telecommuncations and control systems and hardware and software engineering.

I appreciate the diversity of the courses that were offered at the University of Toronto and the enriching education that I received therein. My interests in software development were revived amidst my Professional Experience Year (PEY) when I worked as a 16 month RAMS Engineering Specialist after completing my third year. Thus, I spent the next few years learning HTML, CSS, PHP and Javascript to add to my knowledge of Java , C and C++.

I have always had a deep passion for learning. Learning a new concept amazed to me. It would allow me discover new methods, overcome challenges and ideally gain exposure to different perceptions. It brought with it a sense of adventure and I loved that. To me that was fun. That was exciting. Ultimately, that was how I preferred to spend my time.
